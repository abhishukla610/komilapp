## Introduction

Goods and Services Tax (GST) compliance remains one of the most important responsibilities for small business owners in India. Whether you run a local retail shop, a freelance consulting practice, or a growing e-commerce business, understanding your GST obligations can save you from hefty penalties and keep your business running smoothly.

This guide walks you through the essentials of GST filing for the financial year 2025-26, covering the key returns you need to file, their deadlines, and practical tips to make the process painless.

## Understanding GSTR-1: Your Sales Return

GSTR-1 is a monthly or quarterly return that captures all your outward supplies (sales). Every registered taxpayer must file this return, regardless of whether any sales were made during the period.

**What to include in GSTR-1:**

- B2B (Business-to-Business) invoices with full details including buyer GSTIN
- B2C (Business-to-Consumer) large invoices exceeding Rs. 2.5 lakh
- Credit and debit notes issued during the period
- Exports and supplies to SEZ units
- Advances received against future supplies

**Filing frequency:**

- Businesses with annual turnover above Rs. 5 crore must file monthly by the 11th of the following month
- Smaller businesses can opt for the QRMP (Quarterly Return Monthly Payment) scheme and file quarterly

**Common mistakes to avoid:**

- Mismatching invoice numbers between your books and GSTR-1
- Forgetting to report credit notes
- Incorrect HSN codes for your products or services

## Understanding GSTR-3B: Your Summary Return

GSTR-3B is a self-declared summary return filed monthly or quarterly. It summarizes your output tax liability, input tax credit (ITC) claims, and the net tax payable.

**Key sections of GSTR-3B:**

1. **Outward supplies** - Total taxable value and tax collected
2. **Inter-state supplies** to unregistered persons and composition dealers
3. **Eligible ITC** - Input tax credit available for the period
4. **Ineligible ITC** - Credits that must be reversed
5. **Tax payment** - Net tax after adjusting ITC, paid through cash or credit ledger

**Filing deadlines for GSTR-3B:**

- Monthly filers: 20th of the following month
- QRMP scheme filers: 22nd or 24th of the month following the quarter (depending on your state)

## Penalties for Late Filing

The GST portal does not forgive delays. Here is what late filing costs you:

- **Late fee**: Rs. 50 per day (Rs. 25 CGST + Rs. 25 SGST) for returns with tax liability. For nil returns, it is Rs. 20 per day.
- **Interest**: 18% per annum on the outstanding tax amount, calculated from the due date.
- **Maximum late fee cap**: Rs. 5,000 per return for GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B (combined CGST and SGST).

These penalties add up quickly. A single missed GSTR-3B with Rs. 50,000 in tax liability can cost you thousands in interest alone over a few months.

## Practical Tips for Smooth GST Filing

### 1. Reconcile Your Books Monthly

Do not wait until the filing deadline. Match your purchase register with GSTR-2B (auto-generated ITC statement) every month. This catches mismatches early and ensures you claim the correct ITC.

### 2. Use Accounting Software

Manual GST filing is error-prone. Tools like Tally, Zoho Books, or ClearTax automate invoice matching, HSN classification, and return generation. The investment pays for itself in saved time and avoided penalties.

### 3. Keep Digital Copies of All Invoices

The GST department can request documentation going back several years. Maintain organized digital records of all purchase and sales invoices, along with bank statements and payment proofs.

### 4. File Nil Returns on Time

Even if you had no transactions in a period, you must file nil returns. Skipping nil returns attracts the same late fees and can block your ability to file subsequent returns.

### 5. Track the ITC Reversal Rules

Input tax credit must be reversed in specific situations, such as when a supplier fails to file their return or when goods are used for exempt supplies. Stay on top of Rule 37, 42, and 43 reversals to avoid notices.

### 6. Review GSTR-2B Before Filing GSTR-3B

GSTR-2B is your auto-drafted ITC statement generated from your suppliers' filings. Always cross-check it against your purchase records before claiming ITC in GSTR-3B. Claiming excess ITC is a common trigger for departmental scrutiny.
