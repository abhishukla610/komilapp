## Introduction

Every year during tax season, salaried individuals and professionals face the same question: should I go with the old tax regime or the new one? With the government actively pushing the new tax regime as the default option from FY 2023-24 onwards, understanding the differences between the two has never been more important.

This guide breaks down both regimes for FY 2025-26, with practical examples to help you make an informed decision.

## The New Tax Regime: Lower Rates, Fewer Deductions

The new tax regime offers reduced tax slab rates but removes most exemptions and deductions. For FY 2025-26, the new regime is the default option. You need to actively opt out if you want to use the old regime.

**New Tax Regime Slabs (FY 2025-26):**

| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 3,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 3,00,001 - Rs. 7,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs. 7,00,001 - Rs. 10,00,000 | 10% |
| Rs. 10,00,001 - Rs. 12,00,000 | 15% |
| Rs. 12,00,001 - Rs. 15,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs. 15,00,000 | 30% |

**Key benefit:** A standard deduction of Rs. 75,000 is available for salaried individuals. Additionally, a rebate under Section 87A makes income up to Rs. 7,00,000 effectively tax-free.

**What you lose:** Most deductions under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, 80E, etc.), HRA exemption, LTA exemption, and interest on housing loan for self-occupied property (Section 24b).

## The Old Tax Regime: Higher Rates, More Deductions

The old regime keeps its traditional slab structure but allows you to claim the full range of deductions and exemptions that Indian taxpayers have relied on for decades.

**Old Tax Regime Slabs (FY 2025-26):**

| Income Range | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs. 2,50,000 | Nil |
| Rs. 2,50,001 - Rs. 5,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs. 5,00,001 - Rs. 10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs. 10,00,000 | 30% |

**Deductions available under the old regime:**

- **Section 80C** - Up to Rs. 1.5 lakh (PPF, ELSS, LIC, EPF, tuition fees)
- **Section 80D** - Health insurance premiums (Rs. 25,000 for self, Rs. 50,000 for senior citizen parents)
- **Section 80E** - Interest on education loans (no upper limit)
- **Section 24(b)** - Home loan interest up to Rs. 2 lakh for self-occupied property
- **HRA Exemption** - Based on rent paid, salary, and city of residence
- **LTA** - Leave Travel Allowance exemption
- **Standard Deduction** - Rs. 50,000 for salaried individuals

## Side-by-Side Comparison: A Practical Example

Let us compare both regimes for a salaried individual earning Rs. 12,00,000 per annum.

**Assumptions:**
- Gross salary: Rs. 12,00,000
- Section 80C investments: Rs. 1,50,000
- Health insurance (80D): Rs. 25,000
- HRA exemption eligible: Rs. 1,20,000
- Home loan interest (24b): Rs. 2,00,000

### Under the Old Regime:

- Gross Income: Rs. 12,00,000
- Less Standard Deduction: Rs. 50,000
- Less HRA Exemption: Rs. 1,20,000
- Less 80C: Rs. 1,50,000
- Less 80D: Rs. 25,000
- Less 24(b): Rs. 2,00,000
- **Taxable Income: Rs. 6,55,000**
- Tax: Rs. 5,000 (5% on Rs. 2,50,001-5,00,000) + Rs. 31,000 (20% on Rs. 5,00,001-6,55,000) = **Rs. 36,000**
- Add Cess (4%): Rs. 1,440
- **Total Tax: Rs. 37,440**

### Under the New Regime:

- Gross Income: Rs. 12,00,000
- Less Standard Deduction: Rs. 75,000
- **Taxable Income: Rs. 11,25,000**
- Tax: Rs. 20,000 + Rs. 30,000 + Rs. 18,750 = **Rs. 68,750**
- Add Cess (4%): Rs. 2,750
- **Total Tax: Rs. 71,500**

**Verdict for this example:** The old regime saves approximately Rs. 34,000 in taxes. However, this assumes the taxpayer is maximizing all available deductions.

## When the New Regime Works Better

The new regime tends to be more beneficial when:

1. **You have minimal investments or deductions** - If you do not invest in 80C instruments, do not pay rent (no HRA), and have no home loan, the lower slab rates of the new regime will likely result in less tax.

2. **Your income is below Rs. 7 lakh** - The Section 87A rebate makes income up to Rs. 7 lakh tax-free under the new regime, which is a higher threshold than the old regime.

3. **You prefer simplicity** - The new regime eliminates the need to track and document various deductions, making tax filing straightforward.

4. **You are a senior citizen** - Senior citizens who do not have significant deductions often benefit from the wider slabs.

## When the Old Regime Works Better

Stick with the old regime when:

1. **You have a home loan** - The Rs. 2 lakh deduction under Section 24(b) combined with principal repayment under 80C can significantly reduce taxable income.

2. **You pay substantial rent** - HRA exemption can be one of the largest deductions for people living in metro cities.

3. **You maximize 80C and 80D** - If you are disciplined about tax-saving investments and have health insurance for yourself and parents, the deductions add up quickly.

4. **Your total deductions exceed Rs. 3.75 lakh** - As a rough rule of thumb, if your combined deductions and exemptions exceed Rs. 3.75 lakh, the old regime is likely better.

## How to Switch Between Regimes

- **Salaried individuals** can switch between regimes every financial year. Inform your employer at the start of the year, or choose at the time of filing your ITR.
- **Business owners and professionals** (with business income) who want to opt for the old regime can do so, but switching back to the new regime is limited. Consult a tax professional before making this choice.
